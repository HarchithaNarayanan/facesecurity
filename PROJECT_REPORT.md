## Step 2: What is the problem?

Current authentication methods face critical challenges:

- **Password Fatigue**: Users create weak, reused passwords across multiple platforms
- **Security Vulnerabilities**: Passwords can be stolen, guessed, or phished
- **Single-Factor Weakness**: Traditional systems rely on "something you know" alone
- **Spoofing Attacks**: Basic facial recognition can be fooled by photos or videos
- **User Friction**: Complex security measures frustrate users and reduce adoption

### Why is this problem important to solve?

Authentication is the gateway to all digital services. Weak authentication leads to:
- Data breaches costing billions annually
- Identity theft affecting millions of users
- Loss of trust in digital platforms
- Regulatory compliance failures

A cognitive-aware biometric system solves these issues by combining "something you are" (face) with "something you do" (gesture), creating a virtually unbreakable authentication layer.

After researching and interviewing users:

- Many users said they reuse passwords because they can't remember unique ones for each service
- Security professionals emphasized that biometrics alone can be spoofed
- Users want security that's both strong AND convenient
- Industry reports show 80% of breaches involve weak or stolen credentials
